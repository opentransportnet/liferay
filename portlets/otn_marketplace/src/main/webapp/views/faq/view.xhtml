Frequently Asked Questions


What is OTN's project vision?

The OTN Hub network advances the use of GI and transport-related information by opening, harmonizing and promoting a full range of data to stimulate new service ideas, improve decision-making, create new business opportunities, and enhance the quality of applications and services in the transport domain.

Why are OTN Hubs a popular resource in the transport industry?

The Hubs are a popular resource in the transport industry for a number of reasons:
  • Firstly, the Hubs pull together, harmonize, link and visualize open data including Geospatial Information (GI) from across Europe, including major public datasets such as INSPIRE and Copernicus (Earth Observation) data, real-time sensor data and citizen-volunteered information such as Open Street Map, to provide the most comprehensive data-mapping infrastructure available for the industry, scientists, public servants or citizens. Users can simply chose the geographical locations of the data sets they wish to experiment with. Similarly, new cities can easily create a hub in their local area and join the growing network of providers with a few clicks.
  • Secondly, the user-friendly interface and intuitive data tools, enhanced by location-based services using Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNNS) including Galileo, mean that users do not need to be GI expert or data analysts in order to link data, view the results and unlock insights. Everyone has the same opportunity to be entrepreneurial in his or her approach to new service ideas.
  • Thirdly, the large online peer community supports each other, sharing innovation updates, skills and resources for the development of new service solutions.
  • Lastly, OTN’s business mentoring consultancy helps translate innovative service ideas into reality.

Who does OTN address to?

  1. Data owners. Thanks to projects like OTN, GI and transport-related data owners understand the value that can be unlocked from their data when shared and combined/linked with other data streams. New GI and transport-related data is regularly opened to the Hubs, increasing the value of the ecosystem exponentially.
  2. Cities and Regions. Public Administrations use the Hubs to enhance their own operations, planning and decision making through creating linked data visualizations to generate insights. They use the community to source new service solutions and resources to increase the quality of life in the cities.
  3. Citizens and Businesses. SME’s use the Hubs to stimulate innovative thinking, test new ideas with the Community, and access business consultancy. They are engaged through the simple interface that enables them to play and have fun experimenting with data sets.
  4. Developers. Developers come to the OTN Hubs to have access to the raw materials they need to create new applications and services and to interact with the Cities, regions and SME’s who may commission or buy their innovations.
